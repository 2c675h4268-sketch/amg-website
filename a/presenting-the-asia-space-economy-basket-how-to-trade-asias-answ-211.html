Presenting The "Asia Space Economy" Basket: How To Trade Asia's Answer To SpaceX

Source: ZeroHedge  ·  June 23, 2026

ZeroHedge outlines an investment basket of Asian space economy stocks positioned as regional competitors to SpaceX as the commercial space sector expands globally. The analysis builds on earlier coverage of data centers in space and the growing commercial infrastructure supporting orbital operations. Investors are increasingly looking to Asian space companies as a counterweight to SpaceX's dominant market position.

The Atomic take

The framing of Asian space firms as a counterweight to SpaceX matters because it pushes investors to price launch, satellite, and orbital infrastructure capacity as a regional bet rather than a single-company story, particularly as proposals like data centers in space expand what "space economy" means. Watch whether the basket leans on established players like China's commercial launch entrants and Japanese and Indian satellite firms, or stretches to include speculative names with little flight heritage. The real test is whether any of these companies can demonstrate reusable launch economics or recurring orbital revenue, since that is what underpins SpaceX's lead in the first place.
