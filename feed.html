Artificial Intelligence

Anthropic Stacks Talent and Enterprise Wins as Content Deals Multiply

A U.S. official confirmed Anthropic's Mythos model identified vulnerabilities in highly sensitive U.S. government systems during testing, and Anthropic unveiled Claude Tag, a Slack-based agent positioned as a virtual employee. Nobel laureate John Jumper is leaving Google DeepMind for Anthropic, per Reuters and CNBC. Separately, Google announced a $75 million investment in A24, Getty Images surged on a deal with OpenAI, Kazakhstan signed accords with startup Firebird that could bring up to $10 billion in Nvidia-supported computing investment, and Amazon shelved its OpenAI film 'Artificial' despite its reported $50 billion stake in OpenAI.

Robotics

Humanoids Near Production as GM Triggers Labor Backlash

Hyundai Motor Group plans to buy SoftBank's remaining 9.65% stake in Boston Dynamics for $325 million, taking full ownership as the Atlas humanoid reaches production, according to Reuters citing a newspaper report. GM installed dozens of new robot arms at its Detroit EV plant while 1,300 workers remain laid off, drawing warnings from the UAW at its Constitutional Convention. Separately, Sanctuary AI reported a 99.5% success rate on a wire-plugging task at factory speed for a Tier 1 auto supplier, and NVIDIA introduced Halos, a safety platform for autonomous and humanoid robots working near people.

Space

SpaceX Round-Trips Its IPO As Operations Push Ahead

SpaceX shares, public since a June 12 IPO that briefly pushed its valuation past Amazon and made Elon Musk the world's first trillionaire, fell roughly 16% Monday in a $400 billion selloff before stabilizing near $156, erasing about $600 billion in market value and dropping Musk's net worth to approximately $957 billion per Bloomberg's Billionaires Index. Bloomberg also reported SpaceX took on billions in new debt while cutting its annual interest burden, and Ars Technica reported the company is set to test Starfall, a saucer-shaped reentry vehicle for orbital cargo delivery, on a Falcon 9. Separately, Firefly Aerospace is expected to secure a $110 million US EXIM loan for Texas spacecraft production, India released an expression of interest to transfer PSLV technology to private firms, and NASA's inspector general warned US spaceports are nearing operational capacity.

Longevity

Capital Rewards Platforms, Punishes Single-Asset Clinical Bets

uniQure priced an upsized $225 million Nasdaq offering for its gene therapy pipeline, while Elicio Therapeutics shares fell roughly 73% after its pancreatic cancer therapy missed a mid-stage endpoint, against a stated cash runway of about $15 million through Q4 2026. A Delaware federal jury ordered Amgen subsidiary Teneobio to pay Harbour Antibodies $20.2 million for antibody patent infringement, Argentina repealed restrictions on biotech patents, and Nvidia executive Kimberly Powell pitched agentic AI for drug discovery at the Bio International Convention in San Diego.

Energy & Compute

Capital And Regulators Move To Clear AI Compute Bottlenecks

South Korea's government is in talks with Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix to accelerate semiconductor fab construction by more than 10 years, targeting 2034-2035, per Reuters. FERC unanimously approved orders meant to expedite data center grid connections, while Abu Dhabi-backed MGX is reportedly exploring an acquisition of Singapore's DayOne. On the funding side, Odyssey AI raised $310 million at a $1.45 billion valuation backed by Amazon, AMD Ventures, and Google, and Europe's TensorX raised 8 million euros to buy Nvidia Blackwell B300 GPUs for GDPR-compliant inference.
